Art Deco

Distinguished by its geometric shapes, bold colors, and luxurious materials, Art Deco emerged in the 1920s and 1930s. As a style of interior design, Art Deco is known for its emphasis on opulence and glamour, incorporating resources like chrome, glass, and exotic woods, as well as bold geometric patterns.

This style is known for its emphasis on luxury, glamour, and sophistication, and is a popular choice for those seeking to add a touch of opulence to their homes.

Mid-century

In the mid-20th century, Mid-Century Modern emerged as a design style characterized by clean lines, organic forms, and an emphasis on functionality. Using simple, uncluttered spaces and natural materials to create a warm, welcoming atmosphere, Mid-Century Modern interior design is known for its minimalist and practical approach.

In today's homes, Mid-Century Modern interior design continues to be a popular style for those who wish to create a warm and welcoming environment.

Scandinavian

Scandinavian interior design is characterized by simplicity, functionality, and a focus on natural materials. It originated in the Nordic countries of Europe. Light, neutral colors create a warm, welcoming atmosphere in this style, which is known for its minimalist and practical approach.

Known for its simple, functional, and practical approach, Scandinavian interior design is popular with those looking to create a calming, warm, and inviting interior space.

Plants

The presence of plants can give a room a sense of life and vitality, as well as provide a number of physical and mental health benefits. As a result of their aesthetic benefits, plants can be used as a tool in interior design to create an environment that is welcoming, warm, and healthy. No matter how they are used, plants add life and vitality to any space, whether they are used separately or in conjunction with other elements.
